# Synthetic Walkthrough

> **Fully invented scenario.** Names, roles, and events here are fictional, used only to show the method end-to-end. Do not treat any of it as a real situation.

A user opens with: *"There's a reorg merging my design team into the product org and I keep clashing with the new design manager. Help me think through it."* That triggers the skill. No existing `world_model.md` is found, so we start at Phase 0.

---

## Phase 0 — the contract

> "Let's build a model of your board and then simulate moves against it. One rule I'll hold throughout: I'll separate what each person *does* (observable) from *why you think they do it* (inferred). Motive is where we're least certain and where these scenarios usually turn — so I'll mark it."

## Phase 1 — structure first

```
Design org                         Product org
  Omar (Design Director)             Lena (VP Product)
   └─ Priya (Design Mgr, NEW)         └─ Tess (PM, "Aurora" pod)
        └─ Jordan  ← the user             └─ Raj (Eng Lead)
        └─ Marco (Sr Designer)
                                    (reorg: Priya's team folds under Lena)
Jordan is matrixed into Aurora ...... dotted line to Tess
```

## Phase 2 — people (observed / inferred)

**Jordan (user)** — `[Observed]` Ships strong work, argues hard in critiques, has pushed back on Priya twice in standups. `[Inferred]` Believes craft should win on merit. ⚠️ Reads own bluntness as "just being rigorous."

**Priya (new manager)** — `[Observed]` Reorganized the critique format week one; praised Marco's work publicly, not Jordan's. `[Inferred]` "She's threatened by me and favoring Marco." ⚠️ *load-bearing and almost entirely inference.*
- Competing read: Priya is new and managing up to Lena, so she's amplifying whoever's work is easiest to show off this month — would do this to anyone. Distinguisher: does she praise Jordan's work once it's review-ready, or never?

**Marco (peer)** — `[Observed]` Friendly, fast, politically fluent. `[Inferred]` Quietly positioning as the team's lead designer.

**Tess (PM, ascending)** — `[Observed]` Drives the Aurora roadmap; Lena visibly backs her; likes Jordan's work and says so. `[Inferred]` Self-interested but fair; will champion whatever makes Aurora ship. ✅ keystone.

**Omar / Lena** — opaque; ratings flow Omar→(soon)Lena. Low read on both.

## Phase 3 — dynamics

Reorg folds Priya's team under Lena within ~5 weeks. Decision model shifts from craft-led (Omar) to roadmap-led (Lena/Tess). Jordan's **action stance**: head down, "let the work speak," win critiques on merit. ⚠️ That stance spends the *team's* goodwill every time a critique turns into a Jordan-vs-Priya standoff.

## Phase 4 — synthesis

- **Structural seam:** Jordan's *rating* will route through a chain they can't read (Priya→Lena), while Jordan's *relevance* routes through a roadmap they don't own (Tess/Aurora). Load-bearing point between two misaligned chains.
- **Keystone:** Tess. She's ascending, she already values Jordan's work, and her success metric (Aurora ships well) is the one thing that can make Jordan indispensable *through* the reorg rather than despite it.
- **What does NOT get solved:** making work load-bearing for Tess routes *around* the Priya rating problem; it doesn't *cure* it. And it's concentration risk — if Tess's pod gets deprioritized, the protection goes with it.

## Phase 5 — open questions (resolved with the user)

1. Exposure to Tess — *resolved:* direct, warm, Tess would advocate but serves Aurora first.
2. Jordan's real target — *resolved:* mixed. Says "I just want to do great work," admits a real pull toward being recognized as *the* lead designer. (Revealed-preference territory — don't force the choice; let branches surface it.)
3. Timeline — *resolved:* ~5 weeks to the reorg.

## Phase 6 — branches

| Branch | Target it serves | Core move | Main risk |
|--------|------------------|-----------|-----------|
| A. Win on merit, head down | craft pride | keep doing the work, ignore politics | reorg lands without you positioned; "let the work speak" is the *riskiest* option on a 5-week clock |
| B. Make work load-bearing in Aurora | relevance + recognition | tie your output to Tess's top roadmap bet | concentration risk on Tess |
| C. Repair with Priya | rating safety | stop the standup friction, manage up to her | slow; doesn't fix relevance |

**User then asks a posture counterfactual:** *"What if I stop caring so much about being right in every critique?"*

Decompose the bundle: that's **attachment-to-outcome** + **conviction/judgment**. The valuable version is *attachment-gone, conviction-intact*. Traced through the board: it removes the friction Priya can use to cast Jordan as combative (reactance in reverse); it makes Jordan the easy partner for a roadmap-driven operator like Tess; it stops burning the team's goodwill in standups; it reads as lead-level maturity to Lena.

Then the honest caveats: equanimity is a **posture, not an action** — calm-but-passive still loses the 5-week window if Jordan goes quiet. So pair it with Branch B's move (bring the strong critique points to Tess directly, before the meeting, framed as "what makes Aurora ship better"). And keep the conviction — drop the *attachment*, not the *fire*, or Jordan erases the very edge that makes the work worth championing. The posture makes the move land; the move makes the calm safe.
